What Is This?

Not therapy. Not circling.
Something you practice.

Pattern Break is a live, group practice space where you get to experiment with how you show up with other people. It's spicy, it's exciting, and it's probably different from anything you've done before.

Think of it as exposure therapy for group human dynamics — a place to notice the patterns that run you, try on their opposites, and discover that you have far more choice than you thought in how you relate.

The main room is 80% practice and awareness, 20% emotional processing. It's not primarily a holding-space, although that happens naturally. If you need holding, you ask for it directly — and you'll be met.

Who Is This For?

You have an inkling there's a different way.

You've had a taste of deep connection — at a retreat, in a moment — and you can't replicate it in daily life.

You're bored, or tired, or disconnected in your relationships and you sense there's a richness you haven't found the door to yet.

You're curious to discover choices you didn't know were choices — the flavor of relationship you invite, the flavor of life you live into.

You're hungry to experience something new with other people, and willing to risk being seen learning.

You're an explorer of heart, mind, and body — not looking for answers so much as what becomes possible when you stop running your defaults.

You want friendships built on mutual growth and actually owning what you want with each other.

Premises of the Space

What feels "natural" just means
"practiced for a very long time."

◇

Identity Is Practiced

"Someone who IS quiet" or "someone who IS outgoing" — these aren't fixed truths. They're patterns rehearsed so long you forgot they were learned. What you practice, you become.

◇

Expression Has Impact

Every expression you make toward another — or withhold — lands somewhere. Taking the lens that it's intentional gives you the power to observe your effects and choose differently.

◇

"Authentic" ≠ Fixed

Authentic is often code for "this is what I've always done." The greatest impediment to learning is the belief that something can't be altered or rerouted. Your personality isn't the cage — identification with it is.

◇

States Shape Choices

In a certain emotional state, your range of possible actions narrows. Learning to shift states — without fully buying into the one you're in — makes new choices available.

"If you hate being stuck more than you hate being prodded — you'll come alive here."
Your First Session

Just observe. That's enough.

Arrive however you are

Camera on or off — your choice. You're welcome to simply watch and feel into the space for the entire first session. Zero pressure to perform.

Expect the unexpected

You may be invited into breakout rooms. People may interact with you in ways that feel unfamiliar. This is a different culture than the mainstream — and different from circling and other emotional-processing spaces too.

Notice your defaults

Many people arrive in "empathy mode" — ready to be soft, supportive, hold space. That's lovely, but it's not the main event. Notice what mode you arrive in. That noticing is the beginning of the work.

Make clear, direct requests

If you need space held for you, ask directly. If you want to hold space for someone, name that. Clear requests are the practice. You'll be paired, redirected, or met — never left hanging.

What You'll Practice

The menu is wide. You choose what you work on.

⟐

Pattern Breaks

Identify your defaults, find their 180° opposite, and practice the stretch — first in imagination, then in role play, then live.

⟡

Relational Range

Try on archetypes, opposites, characters, ways of being that aren't "you." Feel what it's like. Expand what's available.

◈

Desire & Aliveness

Practice noticing, owning, and outing what you actually want — despite what others might say or do. Follow your own aliveness.

◎

Impact Awareness

Discover whether the impact you're having on others matches what you intend — or if it's wildly different.

◇

Emotional Tolerance

Build capacity for the feelings that run you — fear, rejection, inadequacy, hopelessness — so they become navigable, not paralyzing.

△

Having Fun

Seriously. Joy and play aren't separate from the deep work — they're the engine of it.

A Note on Culture

This is not a soft space.
It's a brave one.

What This Space Is

An experimental playground for relating. A place to practice self-discontinuity — doing something that isn't "like you." Where being prodded is a gift, because it shows you edges you can't see on your own.

A space where you can be terrified and do the thing anyway. Where someone might not like your personality — and that might be the most liberating experience of your life.

What This Space Isn't

It's not primarily for emotional processing (though that naturally happens, often in breakouts). It's not a space where you must be ready for anger — you always choose what you practice.

It's not mainstream. It's not circling. It's probably unlike anything you've experienced. And if it's too much, honoring that is just as valued as staying.

The Framework

Where this comes from

Predictive Processing

Your brain is a prediction machine — constantly generating expectations about what will happen next, then updating based on what actually occurs. Most of what you experience as "reality" is actually your brain's best guess.

Patterns aren't bugs; they're features. They're your nervous system's way of predicting efficiently. The problem is when predictions become prisons — when the brain keeps predicting the same relational dynamics even when the situation has changed.

Spiritual Insight + Psychological Theory

This work draws on attachment theory, schema therapy, IFS, somatic experiencing, and contemplative traditions that point to the same truth: identity is constructed, and what's constructed can be reconstructed.

The deepest spiritual insight and the best psychological research converge: you are not your patterns. You're the awareness that can notice them, and the agency that can choose differently.
