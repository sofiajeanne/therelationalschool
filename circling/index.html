My practice of circling is...

Building the capacity to be with what is,
in ever deepening + widening circles.

It’s about real-ing, not healing.

Circling is a group practice — one that requires individuals to remain fully themselves. Each group is a completed puzzle. Each individual is a perfect puzzle piece in that puzzle, exactly as they are.

Circling is about building capacity to be with what is. It’s not about connection, resolution, or future objectives, although each of these may (or may not) occur. It’s about discovering what is — or isn’t — already here.

The Five Principles

The 5 principles of circling are guiding lights towards deeper connection. We use them to stay present and connected in circling — or other contexts.

1Stay At The Level Of Sensation

This is an invitation to stay with what’s happening inside of the body.

This principle allows us to be more present, to own our experience in a deeper way, and to more fully experience what happens for us in connection. We often climb up the “ladder of abstraction” without realizing we have done so, and this principle invites a bottom-up understanding of our own experience.

2Trust Your Experience

Trusting our experience allows us to say “yes” to whatever arises within us, with the understanding that it will inevitably shed light on our own and each others processes.

Everything we experience holds something important for us. Believing that there is wisdom in an emotion, or trusting the unknown of what we are experiencing, is a powerful opportunity for transformation.

3Own Your Experience

Owning our experience means we take responsibility for what we are feeling and thinking, rather than making others responsible for what we are experiencing.

This does not mean we are not impacted by others, but that what we feel or think is also our responsibility.

4Commit To Connection

Committing to connection reminds us that when challenging feelings or experiences arise within, we can choose to explore those feelings in connection instead of turning away from the experience or the group.

It does not mean forcing openness, but instead connecting from whatever is actually here.

5Be With The Other In Their World

This principle involves exploring the experience of another person to understand their subjective experience of the present moment.

It requires setting aside our own assumptions about their experience, whether it is similar to ours or not, and being open to what it is like to be them.
